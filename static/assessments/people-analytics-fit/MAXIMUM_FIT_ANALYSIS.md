# Maximum Fit Score Analysis for People Analytics Fit Test

## Can You Get 100% Fit?

**Short Answer:** It's theoretically possible but practically very difficult due to the discrete nature of responses (1-5 scale) and the decimal values in the occupation profile.

## How Fit is Calculated

The fit score uses **Pearson correlation** between your domain scores and the People Analyst occupation profile. The composite fit is a 50/50 weighted average of:
- **Soft Features Fit** (50%): Interests, Values, Personality
- **Learning Interest Fit** (50%): Knowledge Interest, Skills Interest

For 100% fit, you'd need a correlation of **1.0** (perfect positive correlation) in all domains.

## The Challenge

The People Analyst occupation profile has decimal values (e.g., Investigative = 4.92, Social = 4.13), but your responses are integers (1-5). Domain scores are averages of multiple items, so you can get decimal averages, but matching the exact occupation profile pattern is difficult.

## Practical Maximum

Due to rounding and the discrete nature of responses, the practical maximum is likely around **95-98%** rather than exactly 100%. This would require:
1. Perfect pattern matching across all domains
2. Strategic use of response averages to approximate decimal values
3. All domain correlations near 1.0

## Example: Achieving High Investigative Score

To get Investigative = 4.92 (target), you'd need an average of ~4.92 across all investigative items. With integer responses:
- If you have 12 investigative items, you'd need responses that average to 4.92
- This could be: mostly 5s with some 4s (e.g., 10 items at 5, 2 items at 4 = 4.83 average)
- Or: all 5s except one 4 = (11×5 + 1×4) / 12 = 4.92 ✓

## Conclusion

While 100% is theoretically possible with perfect pattern matching, the practical maximum is likely **95-98%** due to the constraints of integer responses and decimal occupation values. To maximize your score, focus on matching the relative pattern: very high on investigative/social/enterprising/conventional interests, high on knowledge utilization and variety values, strong interest in learning HR/psychology/management knowledge, and high conscientiousness/responsibility personality traits.
